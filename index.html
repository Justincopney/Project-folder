Life of the King

  • August 29, 1958 - Born In Gary, Indiana

  • October 6, 1969 - MJ first joined The Jackson 5 between 1960-63, but made their first debut single "I Want You Back/ Who's Loving You" in 1969

  • October 7, 1971 - MJ debuted his first solo single "Got to Be There" and peaked at number four on the US Billboard Hot 100 charts

  • January 24, 1972 - Just three months after debuting his first single "Got Be There", Motown released his solo ablums named the same title which also includes the single

  • August 10, 1979 - MJ broke into stardum after releasing his album "Off the Wall" which has gone to sell over six million copies, with songs like "Rock With You" and "Dont Stop 'Til You Get Enough" as some of the more pominent of his songs

  • November 29, 1982 - Dropping the best selling album of all time, "Thriller" was credited with breaking racial barriers and turning it into a promotional tool

  • August 31, 1987 - Selling over eight and a half million copies "Bad" is also the title track for the album that hit number one on the Hot 100 charts, and stood the best for two consecutive weeks. While helping propel the success of MTV, "Bad" was also the first album to produce five number-one singles

  • August 17, 1993-September 22, 1994 - After changes to his appearance and lifestyle MJ became a topic of controversy, and he was accused of sexually abusing the child of a family friend. The lawsuit was settled and he was not indicted due to lack of evidence

  • June 13, 2005 - After further accusations sexual assault throughout the years he was tried and acquitted on all fronts. The FBI found no evidence of conduct by MJ. After the trial he spent several months living in Bahrain and Ireland

  • June 25, 2009 - While planning a series of comeback concerts, MJ died due to an overdose of propofol given by his personal physician, Conrad Murray, who was convicted in 2011 of involuntary manslaughter for his involement.

  • July 7, 2009 - A public memorial service was held for Michael Jackson at the Staples Center in Los Angeles, CA where more than an estimated two and a half billon people viewed
